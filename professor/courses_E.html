Computer Security (704811-501)

 

Course Overview

This course will cover the theory and practice of computer and information security, including topics such as network security, systems security, Web security, and cryptography with its applications. After completing this course, students will be able to perform cyber attacks and corresponding defenses. Furthermore, they will be able to analyze, design, and build secure systems that are essential in our daily life.

 

Experimentations

To perform cyber attacks and defenses in real, physical environment which is very similar to, but isolated from our Internet, we provide our testbed which comprises open source networking equipments as well as commercial off-the-shelf ones:

  • CISCO routers (Cisco2911/K9, Cisco1921/K9)
  • CISCO switches (WS-C2960G-24TC-L, WS-C2960-24PC-L, WS-C2960-24TC-L)
  • CISCO APs (AIR-AP1142N-K-K9)
  • Open source routers/switches equipped with NetFPGA cards
  • Traffic generators
  • Operating systems (Ubuntu, CentOS, Backtrack, WinXP, etc.)
  • Server systems (Web/DNS/SIP/SMTP/RTSP server etc.)

We also provide a large emulated type of testbed by joining DETERLab. In this testbed, students can experience the followings:

  • DDoS attacks
  • Worm modeling
  • Exploits, intrusions, forensics
  • Man-in-the middle attacks
  • Botnets

Based on the fundamental concepts of cryptograpy, students can also design and build their own secure systems using OpenSSL toolkit:

  • Setting up a CA (Certificate Authority)
  • Advanced programming with SSL
  • Building symmetric and asymmetric encryption systems
  • Building OTP (one-time password) system
